Response to Heuristic Evaluation

We received several violations that made us realize we needed to include some form of onboarding (one violation of each severity 4, 3, and 2), so we made the below video as our first pass at the onboarding process:

One of our severity 4 violations was that the haptic feedback was confusing. To address this (in addition to adding onboarding), we reframed haptic feedback as pocket mode. The purpose of the haptic feedback - being able to use the vibration as a way to follow along with the route without needing to see the screen - is more clear when it is framed as Pocket Mode.

Our other severity 4 violation was that “there was no clear way to change the destination once you have already embarked on the journey”. We elected to ignore this, especially because their description of the violation indicated that the confusion was at least partly due to the lack of an in-app back button, which is the expected behavior for Android apps.

We received a severity 3 violation that the error notification for going in the wrong direction was unclear. This is addressed primarily by adding onboarding, but we would also like to note that our prototype phone does not support varying the amplitude of vibrations, so in most phones the Pocket Mode feedback is clearer.

It was noted as a severity 2 violation that you cannot preview the next step. We made two ways to do this. You can swipe through the screens detailing your next steps or you can pull up a list of the entire text directions (pictured below).

We also addressed a few minor violations, not described here.
